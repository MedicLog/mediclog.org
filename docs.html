Medication Timers

Never miss a redose window. MedicLog automatically tracks medication timing with intelligent alerts.

  • Automatic Timer Start: Logging a medication starts its timer based on your protocol settings
  • Haptic Alerts: Feel countdown warnings at 30 seconds, 10 seconds, and expiration on your iPhone or Apple Watch
  • Visual Dashboard: See all active timers at a glance with color-coded urgency indicators
  • Expiration Warnings: Get notified when medications like Epi or Lidocaine approach their effective window end
  • Custom Intervals: Configure timer durations per medication to match your protocols

Pro Tip: Enable Watch haptics in Settings for wrist-level timer alerts even when your phone is in your pocket.

Voice Logging

MedicLog is built voice-first because your hands are busy saving lives. Speak naturally and let the app understand.

  • Natural Language: Say "Epi one milligram IV push" and MedicLog logs it correctly
  • Custom Aliases: Train the app to recognize your pronunciation or abbreviations ("Epi" vs "Epinephrine")
  • Noise Filtering: Optimized for noisy ambulance environments with siren and road noise filtering
  • Confirmation Feedback: Audio and haptic confirmation so you know your entry was captured
  • Works Offline: Voice recognition runs entirely on-device, no internet required

Encrypted Sharing

Share patient care records securely between medics, across devices, or with receiving facilities.

  • End-to-End Encryption: All shared data is encrypted before leaving your device using industry-standard encryption
  • QR Code Transfer: Generate a QR code to share the current call with a partner's device instantly
  • Cross-Platform: Share between iOS devices now, Android coming soon
  • No Cloud Required: Data transfers directly between devices via local network when possible
  • Audit Trail: Track who accessed what data and when for compliance requirements

Privacy First: Shared data is never stored on any server. Transfer happens directly between devices or via temporary encrypted relay that cannot read the contents.
