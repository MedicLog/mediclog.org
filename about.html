Built by EMS, for EMS

MedicLog started as a solution to a problem every field provider knows: by the time you're writing your PCR, you're reconstructing a call from memory, contaminated gloves, and whatever you managed to scribble on your wrist.

The Problem

Medication logging in the field is a patchwork of habit and improvisation. Timestamps get estimated. Repeat doses get missed or doubled. Radio reports get assembled from memory at the worst possible moment. Existing documentation tools are designed around hospital workflows — not a moving ambulance with a gloved crew managing a critical patient.

MedicLog is designed to be used during the call, not reconstructed after it. Log medications and procedures in real time, get timer-based redose alerts, and generate a radio report automatically when you're ready to transmit — without pulling your attention off the patient.

Who Built This

My name is Joel J. I'm an active EMT with North Memorial Ambulance Service, working rural Minnesota, and a paramedic student at Ridgewater College. I've spent 25+ years in software and IT before transitioning to EMS, which means I had both the clinical context to understand the problem and the technical background to actually do something about it.

MedicLog is a one-person project. No team, no funding, no venture backing. The iOS app is in active beta testing via TestFlight. Android is in development. Neither platform has shipped to the public yet.

Current status: iOS beta via TestFlight — join at mediclog.org/beta. Android is under active development and not yet available. Both platforms are being built and tested by one developer on personal hardware.

Why No Account, No Cloud

Patient data is patient data. MedicLog stores everything locally on your device. There's no account creation, no cloud sync, no server that can be breached. The cross-device transfer between iOS and Android uses encrypted local transfer — the data never leaves your hands.

This isn't a marketing position. It's an architectural decision made on purpose, from the start.

Agency Deployment

MedicLog isn't just for individual providers. The Agency Tools let a medical director configure the entire formulary — medication names, concentrations, default doses, protocols — and distribute it to every device via a QR code. One configuration, fleet-wide deployment, no manual setup for individual crews.
