Tutorial

Walk through a sample call from dispatch to handoff

This tutorial demonstrates the typical MedicLog workflow during an ALS cardiac call. Each step shows how the app helps you document care without slowing you down.

1

Start New Call

Tap the New Call button when dispatch tones you out. MedicLog automatically timestamps the call start and begins tracking elapsed time. You can add dispatch info later or use voice to capture details en route.

2

Patient Contact

On arrival, tap Patient Contact to log the exact time. Use the quick-entry grid to capture initial vitals: tap BP, enter the values, move to pulse and SpO2. Each entry auto-timestamps.

3

Log Interventions

Your patient is in V-fib. Tap Procedures and select Defibrillation. Log the joules and outcome. The timer for next shock consideration starts automatically.

4

Medication Administration

Tap Medications, select Epinephrine, confirm dose (1mg IV). A 3-minute timer starts with haptic alerts at 30 seconds and 10 seconds remaining. Your Watch buzzes along with your phone.

5

Voice Logging

While performing compressions, raise your Watch and say "Amiodarone 300 IV". MedicLog logs it hands-free. You hear confirmation through your Watch speaker and feel the haptic tap.

6

Timer Alerts

Your Watch vibrates: 30 seconds until next Epi dose. You're ready. At the 3-minute mark, you administer the second dose. Tap to log it and the timer resets automatically.

7

ROSC and Transport

ROSC achieved! Tap the event marker for ROSC. Continue logging vitals during transport. The call timeline shows everything in order with precise timestamps.

8

Hospital Handoff

At the hospital, tap Share to generate a QR code. The receiving nurse scans it and instantly has your complete PCR on their device: timestamps, interventions, vitals, everything.

9

Export and Archive

Back at base, export the call as PDF or sync to your ePCR system. MedicLog keeps a local archive you can search later. All data stays on your device until you choose to share or delete it.

Key Takeaways

Timestamps Are Automatic

Every tap captures the exact time. No more guessing when you pushed that first Epi.

Timers Keep You On Track

Haptic alerts mean you never miss a redose window, even when your eyes are on the patient.

Voice Works When Hands Don't

Gloved hands, compressions, or driving—speak naturally and MedicLog captures it.

Share Securely

QR code sharing gives receiving facilities your complete documentation instantly.
